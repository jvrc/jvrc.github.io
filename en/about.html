Japan Virtual Robotics Challenge

“Japan Virtual Robotics Challenge (JVRC)“ is a very unique competition of the disaster response robot by computer simulation. It is a project organized by New Energy and Industrial Technology Development Organization (NEDO).

The participants compete the robot performance by computer simulation “Choreonoid” on the assumed disaster site.

The top performer will demonstrate on the International Robot Exhibition 2015.

Competition Schedule

Date October 7th (Wed.) – 10th (Sat.), 2015
Place CEATEC JAPAN 2015 site
Makuhari Messe 2-1 Nakase, Mihama-ku, Chiba, Japan
Application Fee free
Qualification

individual, team or corporation (Non-disclosure agreement is available only for corporation)
competition contents

A participant develops the software which controls a robot, works on the task which assumed a disaster site on the simulator and competes for its performance.
Commendation

A high achiever can get the right to load software into the production developed on NEDO business.(It's being considered.)
Organizers New Energy and Industrial Technology Development Organization
Co-Organizer Ministry of Economy, Trade and Industry
