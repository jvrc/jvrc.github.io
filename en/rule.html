1. Introduction: Background for the development of
　 the JRVC competition tasks

　The tunnel disaster was chosen as a scenario for the development of the JRVC (Japan Virtual Robotics Challenge) competition tasks.

　In Japan, the social infrastructures developed during the rapid growth period will become 50 years old after 20 years from now. Appropriate maintenance is necessary to avoid a huge impact on human life and society by collapse or failure of the social infrastructures. However, aging maintenance workers and a lack of skill transfer to younger workers would cause many problems of infrastructure maintenance in Japan. Though periodic inspections of major infrastructure are conducted every few years, this frequency would not be enough to ensure the detection of abnormalities such as rapid deterioration. In addition, there are some areas where it is difficult to inspect visually. During a disaster, rapid restoration is hard to achieve due to the amount of time required to conduct emergency inspection of the social infrastructures. In 2014, these circumstances led to the formation of collaboration between the Ministry of Land, Infrastructure, Transport and Tourism, and the Ministry of Economy, Trade and Industry. This resulted in a research and development project to promote the application of robots to social infrastructure, called “Investigation and Evaluation Project of Robots for Next-Generation Social Infrastructure”. The “NEDO 2014 White Paper on Robotics” also lists the field robotics application such as infrastructure, civil engineering, disaster response and agriculture.
  With aging social infrastructure, robot technology can be effective in sites inaccessible to humans. Such capabilities are similar to those required in the disaster response robots(※1)　。According to the probability of tunnel accidents a year(※2), the application of response robot technology to frequently-used maintenance robots for infrastructure enables the social implementation of the response robots.

   Especially, search robots with monitoring technology in confined areas should be developed intensively, because they also can be used in disaster situation for hazard inspection of confined areas such as under debris, among and within piping and bridges.

(※1) The 2012 Report of Council on Competitiveness Nippon (COCN) ”Robot technology used at disaster sites and its operating system”
(※2) Appendix 4-1 “Subdivisions of future disaster and robot (building and civil engineering infrastructure disaster): Nationwide occurrence frequency and scale” in 2013 COCN Report “Establishment plan for a disaster response robot center”

2. Scenario

  The scenario is set within a tunnel for two way traffic in the mountainous area. The curved tunnel extends for 700m. The disaster scenario is as follows: an earthquake caused the collapse of the tunnel wall, which fell on moving vehicles, causing a crash followed by a multiple pileup of following vehicles. Affected vehicles include a tanker and a large truck. The tanker overturned and the truck scattered its load across the roadway. Some victims remain trapped in their vehicles.


3．Equivalent Tasks

　In the actual inspection tasks, visual inspection is often used and hammering test is also used, and the latter is difficult to computer simulate accurately. When a robot performs the hammering test, the basic task is to maintain the robot end-effector at the specified position and direction relative to the test object. Instead of simulating the hammering test, the simulator replicates a more feasible “equivalent task” to evaluate the same basic technology.

Equivalent tasks in the JVRC are defined as visual inspection of “targets” consisting of QR codes and pipes. The size of QR codes and the length of pipes is one of the followings:

The width of QR code (corresponding diameter = diagonal dimension of QR code) 140mm(200mm)、 35mm(50mm)、 7mm(10mm)
The length of pipe 100mm、50mm、0mm

４．Competition Task

The basic competition procedure is as follows:

１ Several “gates”, which consist of a “blue pole” and a “red pole”, are placed to indicate the path for a robot to take in the field. A robot needs to pass the gate with the blue pole on the left.
２ On top of the pole at each gate, there is a flag with a gate number. The robot needs to pass each gate in a numerical order.
３ Passing the gate is complete when the entire robot passes through the gate.
４ The robot waits behind the start line before the competition starts. The start line is drawn between the poles with the gate number 1. The robot must not go beyond the line until the start time.
５ After the signal to start is given, the robot can go beyond the start line to perform a task.
６ The task is over when the robot passes the end line, or exceeds the time limit. The end line is the line between the poles with the largest gate number.
７ The origin in each field is located in the middle of the start line, indicated by an identifiable marker.

The following code is used for each competition task.
（ task category code ＋ task number ）_（ field condition )

ex: R2-1_M-s　→　condition outside of a standard car in normal condition

4-1.Ordinary task (task category code: O)

Task O1：Visual Inspection

Purpose Conduct visual inspections of cracks on the tunnel wall and road.
Subtasks
  1. ●Conduct an investigation of targets in the tunnel and report the result.
  2. ●The inspection report should be submitted in compliance with the format of the “Inspection Report” in the “Road tunnel regular inspection manual”
  3. ●The following items are to be investigated.

【O1-1】 Cracks on the wall, swell, leakage.
【O1-2】 Cracks on the wall, sag, leakage.

Field Conditions and Specifications

●A tunnel approximately 3.6m in width.

●Targets are placed on the tunnel wall at a height of between 0mm and 2400mm.

●The targets contain information associated with “Road tunnel regular inspection manual.” Read and report the location and the result.
Task Accomplishment Pass the finish line and submit the report of all targets.
Score ● Ranking and scoring are based on the total time to accomplish the task.
● Penalty time is added according to the number of mistakes on targets
　 investigated.

Reference：
The Ministry of Land, Infrastructure, Transport and Tourism, “Road tunnel regular inspection manual”

　

Task O2：Hammering Test

Purpose

Conduct a hammering test on fastener components and damaged wall to check the condition of attachments located near the roof.
Subtasks
  1. ●Conduct an investigation (health diagnostics) of targets located near the attachment of the tunnel and report the result.
  2. ●The inspection report should be submitted in compliance with the format of the “Inspection Report” in the “Road tunnel regular inspection manual.”
  3. ●It is necessary to avoid colliding with the structures during the investigation of targets.
Field Conditions and Specifications

●The tunnel is approximately 3.6m in width．

●The attachment is installed to the tunnel roof with fastener components.

●Targets for inspection are located near the fastener components．

●The targets contain information associated with “Road tunnel regular inspection manual.” Read and report the result.

●The drawing of the tunnel is available．

●A scaffolding has been placed to facilitate access to the roof of the tunnel.
Task Accomplishment Pass the finish line and submit the report of all targets.
Score ● Ranking and scoring are based on the total time to accomplish the task.
● Penalty time is added according to the number of mistakes on targets
　 investigated.
４-２ Task at the outset of disaster/rescue
　　　(Task category code: R)

Task R1：Vehicle Inspection

Purpose Check the appearance of the vehicles and surrounding conditions to examine the vehicle damage, leaking fuel and survivors outside the vehicles.
Subtasks
  1. ●Start from the specified location, locate and inspect the following 3 search categories (1)-(3) of each vehicle, report the result and head to the goal.
  2. ●3 categories are as follows:

【R1-1】Condition outside the vehicle
・Identify 4 targets placed outside the vehicle and report the findings.

【R1-2】 Condition inside the vehicle
・Identify targets placed inside the vehicle and report the findings.

・Targets are placed in the following 5 locations.
【R1-2A】Driver seat
【R1-2B】Passenger seat
【R1-2C】Back seat
【R1-2D】Under the driver or passenger seat
【R1-2E】Under the back seat

【R1-3】 Location of the vehicle

  1. ●Report the quantitative location of targets placed outside the vehicle. Quantitative location means 3D coordinate (x, y, z) of the target location.
  2. ●Submit 3D information describing the vehicles' qualitative condition.
Field Conditions and Specifications Abandoned vehicle type is either
【M】standard size car
【L】large size car and vehicle condition is either
【s】stationary (tires are in contact with the road surface)
【u】overturned.
Task Accomplishment
  1. ●Execute some or all of the item 【R1-1】, 【R1-2】 and 【R1-3】 and head to the goal.
  2. ●Report the condition of targets for the item 【R1-1】and 【R1-2】.
  3. ● Report each target's 3D coordinate and 3D information of vehicle condition for item 【R1-3】．
Score ● Points are given based on identification and report of targets.
● Additional points (bonus points) are given according to the time when all of the subtasks are completed.

Task R2：Traversing Obstacles

Purpose Traverse rough terrain.
Subtasks Start from the specified location, traverse obstacles, or in the confined space formed by obstacles, and reach the goal.
Field Conditions and Specifications Traverse 3 different fields along with the specified route.

【R2_A】
Crossing Ramp field
Obstacles consisting of ramps with 150 degree top and valley angle.

【R2_B】
DRC Stepfield
Obstacles consisting of 40-square-cm blocks with 15 degree ramp.

【R2_C】
Narrow Space Field Consists of 900mm modules. Obstacles simulating confined area consisting of 2-by-4 blocks, including a walk-through pipe of 60cm diameter.

Task Accomplishment Traverse from the start to end point.
Score
  1. ●Ranking and scoring are based on the total time to accomplish the task.
  2. ●If the robot fails to reach the goal in time, points are given based on the point it reached (check point). 3 check points are placed including the goal．

Task R3：Vehicle Inspection using Tools

Purpose Investigate condition inside the vehicle by using tools commonly used by humans.
Subtasks ● Investigate the inside condition of the vehicle by using tools.
● The following tools are used and available in the field.

・　Spreader
・　Ladder

● nvestigation tasks are as follows.

【R3_A】
Deformed vehicle
・Pick up the spreader and move to the vehicle.

・Open the vehicle door with the spreader.

・To open the door with the spreader, manipulate the switch while placing the spreader at the specified location and direction.

・Identify a target through the opened door. Report the findings.

【R3_B】
Overturned large vehicle
・Climb the ladder and identify a target through the upper window. Report the findings.

・No need to use a ladder if a robot can investigate through the upper window without using a ladder.

Field Conditions and Specifications ● A spreader is placed on a platform in the field.
● A ladder is already attached to the vehicle.
Task Accomplishment Identify a target within a specified time frame, report the findings and head to the goal.
Score
  1. ●Points are given for the identification of targets and the report.
  2. ●Additional points (bonus points) are given according to the time only when all of the subtasks are completed.

Task R4：Secure the Route

Purpose Remove the obstacles on the specified route to secure the route for operation and evacuation.
Subtasks ● Remove the obstacles from the specified route to secure the route.
● The shape and weight of the obstacles are as follows.

【A】Obstacles to remove is “L” shaped and weighs 3kg.
【B】Obstacles to move is “L” shaped and weighs 10kg.
Field Conditions and Specifications

● The specified route for object removal is designated by color in the field.

● Fixed structures surrounding the specified route impose constraints on the movement of the robots and the obstacles.

Task Accomplishment Remove obstacles from the specified route and head to the goal.
Score
  1. ●Points are given by removing each obstacle from the specified route.
  2. ●Additional points (bonus points) are given according to the time only when all of the subtasks are completed.

Task R5：Support Fire Extinguishing

Purpose Support in fire extinguishing
Subtasks Subtasks are as follows.

【R5-1】Open the door of firefighting equipment cabinet.
【R5-2】Pull out the entire hose.
【R5-3】Remove a nozzle.
【R5-4】Connect the nozzle to the hose.
【R5-5】Open the valve. (Check the water flow)


Field Conditions and Specifications
  1. ●Firefighting equipment is based on “Road tunnel emergency facility installation standards”.
  2. ●Firefighting equipment cabinet is installed on the tunnel wall and the door handle is located 1m high.
  3. ●Door, hinged at the bottom, opens without a lock.
  4. ●Hose is stored in a concertina arrangement.
  5. ●Nozzle and hose are stored separately.
  6. ●Valve is installed inside firefighting equipment cabinet. The valve handle sticks out sideways.

References：
Task Accomplishment Execute some or all of the subtasks 【R5-1】 to 【R5-5】 and head to the goal.
Score
  1. ●Points are given by accomplishing subtasks.
  2. ●Additional points are given according to the time (bonus points) only when all of the subtasks are completed in the following order.
