JAXON（real model）






This robot (JAXON) has been developed by Jouhou System Kougaku (JSK) laboratory, the University of Tokyo.

Technology for this robot is based on the recent research of high-speed and high-torque motor driver with water-cooling system, RTM-ROS inter-operation for intelligent robotics, and generation of full-body fast dancing motion, due to the generic 10 year's research of HRP-2 as a platform humanoid robot.

Development target is the robot support in a variety of unsafe human tasks teleoperated by humans in case of a disaster, equipped with body structure capability for use of human devices and tools in human environment, performance for dynamic full-body actions covering human-sized speed and power, and basic function for intelligent and integrated robot platform system for performing various tasks independently.


It has 8-DOF arms, 6-DOF legs, a 3-DOF torso, and a 2-DOF neck, along with shiftable hands and head-mounted camera.

Name JAXON
Information Height：188cm、　Weight：110kg、　Degree of freedom：33 DOF
Developer Jouhou System Kougaku (JSK) laboratory, the University of Tokyo

Hydra（real model）






Hydra is a 41DOF humanoid robot developped in the Universty of Tokyo, with 8 for each arm, 6 for each leg, 2 for the waist, 1 for the neck. All axes except for the neck are driven by Electro-Hydraulic Actuators (EHA).

The name of "Hydra" was derived from "hydraulics." A hand has four underactuated fingers, each of which except the thumb has only 1DOF for flexion/extension while the thumb has adduction/abduction in addition and 2DOF in total. The geometry and mass properties are not necessarily correspond to the real ones. Each of wrist, ankle, shoulder, and hip joints is a 2DOF universal joint and is driven by closd kinematic chains. The geometric model of such joint is represented by two serial joints with mutually intersecting orthogonal axes. The maximum joint torque of a joint driven by a liniear actuator and a closed kinematic chain varies by the joint angles. The workspace of a 2DOF joint driven by a closed kinematic chain cannot be represented by independent ranges of two joints. The maximum torques and the workspace of joints approximately represents their values.

Name Hydra
Information Height：180cm、　Weight：110kg、　Degree of freedom：41 DOF
Developer The University of Tokyo　Nakamura laboratory and three other organizations

HRP-2kai（real model）

HRP-2 is a humanoid robot developed in the Japanese national project "Humanoid Robotics Project(HRP)"(METI/NEDO, FY1998-2002). The robot was developed by Kawada Industries, Inc., AIST, Yaskawa Electric and Shimizu corporation. HRP-2Kai is its improved version for DARPA Robotics Challenge(DRC). Its legs and arms are extended in order to walk on rough terrain including large gaps and to improve its manipulation ability. Joint configurations of writes are modified and sensors including a LRF and cameras are added. Since the robot was designed more than ten years ago, its electronics system became old and it was difficult to use the latest computers.

To cope with this issue, its internal electronics system is fully re-designed. Although its exterior looks similar to the original one, HRP-2Kai is almost a newly designed robot.

Notice: Signing an NDA (Non-Disclosure Agreement) with the robot builder, Kawada Technologies, Inc. is required to download the robot model. Note that only legal entities can sign the NDA.


Name HRP-2kai
Information Height：170cm、　Weight：70kg、　Degree of freedom：32 DOF
Developer New Energy and Industrial Technology Development Organization
